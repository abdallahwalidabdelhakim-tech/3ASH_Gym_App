# Fact Finding Techniques Report
**Project Name:** 3ASH - Gym & Progress Tracker
**Date:** February 8, 2026

## 1. Introduction
This report outlines the fact-finding techniques employed to gather requirements and understand the user needs for the **3ASH** fitness application. The goal was to identify pain points in current workout tracking methods and determine the essential features for a successful MVP.

---

## 2. Techniques Used

### 2.1 Interviews
**Method:** One-on-one semi-structured interviews were conducted with potential stakeholders.
- **Participants:** 
  - 3 Regular Gym Goers (Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced)
  - 1 Personal Trainer
- **Key Questions:**
  - "How do you currently track your workouts?"
  - "What is the most frustrating part of logging your progress?"
  - "What data (weight, volume, 1RM) is most important to you?"
- **Findings:**
  - Beginners struggle with consistency and knowing "what to do next."
  - Advanced users want quick entry (auto-fill previous weights) and volume analysis.
  - Paper logs are reliable but lack analysis; notes apps are disorganized.

### 2.2 Questionnaires (Surveys)
**Method:** An online survey was distributed to a wider audience via social media fitness groups.
- **Respondents:** 45 Participants.
- **Key Insights:**
  - 78% of users prefer dark mode for gym apps.
  - 65% want a "Rest Timer" integrated into the logger.
  - 90% consider "Progress Charts" a must-have feature.

### 2.3 Observation
**Method:** Shadowing users during their workout sessions (with permission).
- **Environment:** Local commercial gym.
- **Observations:**
  - Users experienced friction when switching between a timer app and a notes app.
  - Users often forgot the weight they lifted in the previous session, leading to guessing.
  - **Conclusion:** The app needs an integrated rest timer and must display "Previous Set" data prominent.

### 2.4 Document Analysis / Competitive Analysis
**Method:** Reviewing existing solutions and competitor applications.
- **Apps Analyzed:** Strong, Hevy, MyFitnessPal.
- **Findings:**
  - *Strong:* Excellent logger, but expensive subscription.
  - *MyFitnessPal:* Great for nutrition, but workout tracking is clunky.
  - **Opportunity:** 3ASH can offer a streamlined, "workout-first" experience with essential metrics for free (or lower cost).

---

## 3. Summary of Requirements
Based on the fact-finding process, the following core requirements were identified:
1.  **Fast Logging:** Minimal clicks to enter weight/reps.
2.  **History Access:** Immediate view of previous performance for the current exercise.
3.  **Visual Progress:** Simple line charts for weight trends.
4.  **Dark Theme:** To save battery and reduce eye strain in gym environments.
5.  **Offline Capability:** Gyms often have poor signal.

---

## 4. Conclusion
The combination of interviews and direct observation provided the most valuable insights regarding the "friction" of current solutions. The proposed 3ASH app will prioritize speed and ease of input to address these findings.
